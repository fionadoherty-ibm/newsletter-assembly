NEWSLETTER DRAFT WIZARD — PLANNING DOCUMENT
============================================
Last updated: June 2025


OVERVIEW
--------
The weekly IBMer newsletter process is manual and time consuming. This tool — built
in IBM Bob — helps the team produce a high-quality, engaging newsletter faster and
with more consistency. Bob supports the work. Editors own the story.

Success means:
  - Stories are submitted in a consistent format every time
  - Titles, abstracts and CTAs are automatically generated and aligned to guidelines
  - Content follows smart brevity and Neverland principles
  - The newsletter structure is easy to assemble
  - Story placement and prioritization are easy to manage and shift
  - Editors spend less time rewriting and more time refining


CONSTRAINTS
-----------
  - Must follow existing newsletter format and structure
  - Must align to editorial guidelines (smart brevity, tone, clarity, no jargon)
  - Must keep humans in the loop for review and approvals
  - Content comes from multiple sources (Box, OneDrive)
  - Not all stories include images in the newsletter
  - The structure must stay consistent week to week
  - Tool should work for nontechnical users
  - Output must be usable for future export to Marketo
  - w3 stories have a variety of topics, length and format


CURRENT NEWSLETTER PRODUCTION PROCESS
--------------------------------------
Prior week:
  - Box note wireframe sent to CorpComms team
  - Story owners identify content they plan to submit

Content submission (due Wednesday 9:00 a.m.):
  - Story owners submit:
      - Engaging story title
      - Story abstract (~500-700 characters)
      - Fun, action-oriented CTA (2-4 words)

Editorial review (Wednesday ~11:00 a.m.):
  - Team reviews all stories on Teams call
  - Discusses relevance, priority, placement and edits
  - Identifies content gaps or missing assets
  - All edits made ASAP after call

Newsletter structure:
  - Publication date (Month Day, Year)
  - "IBMer News This Week. The news you need to know."
  - Hero image with title, abstract and CTA
  - "From the CEO" section with photo, abstract and CTA
  - Remaining sections (vary by week):
      - In the News
      - Featured Stories
      - Strategy Spotlight
      - Latest Updates
  - Always ends with a lighter culture section:
      - Around IBM
      - Just for #fun
      - Occasional event-specific topics
  - Stories arranged by category and business priority
  - Sections that always require a visual:
      - Hero story
      - CEO feature
      - Bottom culture/fun section
  - Images or GIFs sourced from story webpage or DAM

Marketo build and review:
  - Chris (or team member with Marketo access) builds newsletter in Marketo
  - Preview sent to team mid-afternoon Wednesday
  - Team reviews for formatting, content, spelling, grammar, broken links, image placement

Leadership review:
  - Newsletter shared with leadership after team edits
  - Final approval from Jonathan Adisak (Wednesday evening or Thursday morning)

Distribution:
  - Newsletter distributed Thursday, typically mid to late afternoon


GUIDING PRINCIPLE
-----------------
Bob supports the work. Editors own the story.

Where humans stay involved:
  - Content submission: story owners decide what is important, provide raw inputs
  - Editorial review: team reviews all stories together, makes final decisions on
    relevance, priority, placement and messaging. Bob suggests, editors approve.
  - Final approval: editors confirm structure, flow and accuracy before publishing

HOW THE TEAM WORKS INDEPENDENTLY
----------------------------------
Each person works in their own Bob playground folder on their laptop, scoped to
their assigned task. Outputs are combined into a single shared workspace at the end.

Each person's playground should contain:
  - This plan document (read-only reference)
  - Their task's sample inputs (e.g. sample w3 story PDFs, newsletter guidance doc)
  - Their task's prompt(s) built and tested in Bob
  - A sample output file showing what their task produces

The input/output contracts below define the handoff format between tasks so that
no one is blocked waiting for another task to be fully built. Tasks 2, 3, and 4
can all begin in parallel as soon as Task 1 defines its output format.


THE SIX TASKS
=============

TASK 1 — CONTENT INGESTION AND STORY INTAKE
--------------------------------------------
Goal:
  Accept raw story submissions from Box, OneDrive, or direct paste. Normalize them
  into a consistent structured format that every downstream task can reliably consume.

Input:
  - Pasted text from story owner, OR
  - PDF from Box or OneDrive, OR
  - URL to a w3 story page

Output — Structured Story Object:
  {
    "story_id":           unique identifier,
    "source_url":         https://...,
    "raw_title":          original title as submitted,
    "raw_body":           full story text,
    "submitted_by":       story owner name,
    "suggested_category": In the News | Featured Stories | Strategy Spotlight |
                          Latest Updates | Around IBM | Just for #fun,
    "requires_image":     true | false,
    "source_type":        w3 | press release | blog | other
  }

Status: [ ] pending


TASK 2 — TITLE, ABSTRACT AND CTA GENERATOR
--------------------------------------------
Goal:
  Take a normalized story and auto-generate a newsletter-ready title, abstract
  (~500-700 characters), and a 2-4 word action-oriented CTA aligned to editorial
  voice and tone.

Input:
  - Structured Story Object from Task 1

Output — Generated Content Block:
  {
    "story_id":           matches Task 1,
    "generated_title":    newsletter-ready headline,
    "generated_abstract": 500-700 character summary,
    "generated_cta":      2-4 word action phrase,
    "word_count":         000,
    "character_count":    000
  }

Status: [ ] pending


TASK 3 — EDITORIAL RULES ENGINE
---------------------------------
Goal:
  The "bad cop" quality check. Validate generated or submitted copy against smart
  brevity, Neverland, no-jargon, and IBM style rules. Return flagged issues with
  suggested corrections for editor review.

Input:
  - Generated Content Block from Task 2

Output — Editorial Review Report:
  {
    "story_id":  matches Task 2,
    "approved":  true | false,
    "flags": [
      {
        "field":         generated_title | generated_abstract | generated_cta,
        "issue":         description of the problem,
        "rule_violated": smart brevity | no jargon | neverland | IBM style,
        "suggestion":    corrected or improved version
      }
    ],
    "final_title":    approved or editor-revised title,
    "final_abstract": approved or editor-revised abstract,
    "final_cta":      approved or editor-revised CTA
  }

Status: [ ] pending


TASK 4 — IMAGE RECOMMENDATION
-------------------------------
Goal:
  For the three sections that always require a visual (Hero, CEO, culture/fun),
  surface image candidates from the story's web page or DAM. Flag any stories still
  missing assets before assembly begins.

Input:
  - Structured Story Object from Task 1

Output — Image Recommendation Block:
  {
    "story_id":       matches Task 1,
    "requires_image": true | false,
    "recommended_sources": [
      {
        "source_type":   story webpage | DAM,
        "url_or_path":   https://... or DAM asset path,
        "description":   brief description of the image
      }
    ],
    "image_status":   found | not found | needs manual sourcing
  }

Status: [ ] pending


TASK 5 — NEWSLETTER ASSEMBLY AND STORY ORDERING
-------------------------------------------------
Goal:
  Arrange all processed stories into the correct newsletter sections in the right
  sequence. Allow editors to reorder and reprioritize before the output is locked.

Input:
  - Editorial Review Reports from Task 3 (all stories, approved)
  - Image Recommendation Blocks from Task 4

Output — Assembled Newsletter Draft:
  {
    "publication_date": Month Day, Year,
    "headline": "IBMer News This Week. The news you need to know.",
    "sections": [
      {
        "section_name": Hero | CEO | In the News | Featured Stories |
                        Strategy Spotlight | Latest Updates |
                        Around IBM | Just for #fun,
        "stories": [
          {
            "story_id":       ...,
            "final_title":    ...,
            "final_abstract": ...,
            "final_cta":      ...,
            "image":          url or DAM path | none,
            "order":          1
          }
        ]
      }
    ]
  }

Status: [x] done — see index.html (GitHub Pages), task-5-subtask-1-auto-placement-prompt.md, task-5-bob-paste-block.md, task-5-assembly-plan.md


TASK 6 — SUBJECT LINE GENERATOR
---------------------------------
Goal:
  Once the newsletter is assembled, generate candidate email subject lines for the
  distribution send. Subject lines should be punchy, curiosity-driving, and aligned
  to the week's lead stories so the team has options to choose from rather than
  writing from scratch.

Input:
  - Assembled Newsletter Draft from Task 5

Output — Subject Line Options:
  {
    "publication_date": Month Day, Year,
    "subject_line_options": [
      {
        "option":       1,
        "subject_line": ...,
        "style":        curiosity | urgency | direct | playful
      },
      {
        "option":       2,
        "subject_line": ...,
        "style":        ...
      },
      {
        "option":       3,
        "subject_line": ...,
        "style":        ...
      }
    ],
    "recommended": 1
  }

Status: [x] done — see task-6-subject-line-prompt.md, task-6-bob-paste-block.md


HANDOFF CHAIN
=============

  Task 1  -->  Task 2    Structured Story Object
  Task 1  -->  Task 4    Structured Story Object
  Task 2  -->  Task 3    Generated Content Block
  Task 3  -->  Task 5    Editorial Review Report
  Task 4  -->  Task 5    Image Recommendation Block
  Task 5  -->  Task 6    Assembled Newsletter Draft

Tasks 2, 3, and 4 can begin in parallel as soon as Task 1 defines its output format.
Tasks 5 and 6 require all upstream tasks to be complete before combining.
